# India F&O Pulse — 30 Jun 2026: what the day's options & futures positioning is whispering

Nifty and Sensex show a mildly bullish undertone, while Bank Nifty appears poised for a range-bound session.

**TL;DR**
*   **Nifty 50:** The data suggests a mildly bullish bias, with a potential upward pull towards the 24,000 level, despite a nearby Call resistance.
*   **Sensex:** A mildly bullish sentiment seems to be at play, with option writers positioning for a move towards 77,000.
*   **Bank Nifty:** The signals are mixed, indicating a likely range-bound movement between 57,500 and 57,600, with both Call and Put writers establishing strong positions.

## What the indices did today

**Nifty 50** closed the day at 23,865.75, declining by 0.34%. It ended just below its heaviest Call Open Interest (OI) wall at 23,900 but found support near the heaviest Put OI base at 23,850, suggesting a tight range for the session.

**Sensex** saw a similar dip, closing at 76,478.67, down 0.33%. The index settled well below its significant Call OI wall at 77,000, while fresh Put writing activity around 75,500 indicates potential support building up.

**Bank Nifty** also ended in the red, at 57,542.90, a decrease of 0.32%. It concluded the day very close to both its heaviest Call OI at 57,600 and Put OI at 57,500, pointing towards a highly consolidated trading day.

## Decoding today's open interest

Open Interest (OI) represents the total number of outstanding derivative contracts that have not yet been settled. In options, the strike price with the highest Call OI typically acts as a significant resistance level, meaning many traders believe the index may struggle to move above this point. Conversely, the strike with the highest Put OI often serves as a strong support level, where many anticipate the index will find a floor.

For **Nifty 50**, the heaviest Call OI is observed at 23,900, which is just 0.1% above today's spot close. This suggests that 23,900 might act as an immediate ceiling. On the Put side, the heaviest OI is at 23,850, a mere 0.1% below spot, indicating a potential strong base. Today's fresh writing activity saw heavy Call writing near 23,900, reinforcing the idea of a cap there, while fresh Put writing near 23,850 suggests put sellers are confident of this level holding as a floor.

**Sensex** shows its heaviest Call OI at 77,000, which is 0.7% above the spot close, potentially acting as a significant resistance. While the data for the heaviest Put OI wall isn't explicitly provided, fresh Put writing was notably heavy near 75,500, suggesting that option writers are establishing a support base around this level.

In **Bank Nifty**, the heaviest Call OI is at 57,600, just 0.1% above spot, indicating immediate overhead resistance. The heaviest Put OI is at 57,500, also very close to spot (0.1% below), suggesting a strong immediate floor. Today's fresh Call writing was concentrated near 57,700, implying that call sellers expect the index to remain below this level. Simultaneously, fresh Put writing near 57,500 indicates put sellers are banking on this level as a robust support.

## PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The Put-Call Ratio (PCR) is a popular indicator derived by dividing the total Put Open Interest by the total Call Open Interest. A PCR value above 1 generally suggests a bullish sentiment as there are more outstanding Put options than Call options, implying more protection being bought or Puts being sold. Conversely, a PCR below 1 often indicates a bearish bias.

For **Nifty 50**, the PCR based on total Open Interest (PCR(OI)) stands at 0.8, which is considered a balanced book, not leaning strongly in either direction. However, the PCR based on today's fresh flow (ΔPCR) is -1.23, which is a mixed or balanced flow for the day, suggesting no clear directional conviction from fresh option writing.

**Sensex** has a PCR(OI) of 0.78, again indicating a relatively balanced options book. The PCR based on today's flow (ΔPCR) is 0.71, also suggesting a mixed to slightly Call-heavy fresh writing activity for the day.

**Bank Nifty** shows a PCR(OI) of 0.78, reflecting a balanced options book overall. However, its PCR based on today's flow (ΔPCR) is 0.56, which indicates that today's fresh writing activity was predominantly Call-heavy, potentially signaling a bearish tilt from the day's new positions.

## Max pain and the magnet effect

"Max Pain" refers to the strike price at which option writers (those who sell options) would experience the least financial loss if the underlying index were to expire at that specific strike. It's often considered a magnetic point, as the market sometimes tends to gravitate towards this level as expiry approaches, due to the collective positioning of option writers.

For **Nifty 50**, the Max Pain level is at 24,000, which is approximately 0.6% above today's spot close. This suggests a potential upward pull towards this level as expiry nears.

**Sensex** has its Max Pain point at 77,000, about 0.7% higher than its current spot. This could act as an attractive level for the index to gravitate towards.

**Bank Nifty**'s Max Pain is located at 57,800, which is around 0.4% above its spot price. This indicates a mild upward bias in terms of where option writers would prefer the index to settle.

## FII positioning — what the big money did today

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) are significant participants in the Indian derivatives market, and their positioning can offer insights into broader market sentiment. We are looking at yesterday's data (June 29, 2026) as today's NSE archive is not yet published.

FIIs' Index futures long ratio stands at 17.0%, meaning only 17.0% of their total Index futures contracts are long, while the majority are short. Historically, a low FII Index futures long ratio has often coincided with a cautious or bearish outlook from these large participants.

In Stock futures, their long ratio is 54.6%, indicating a slight preference for long positions over short positions in individual stocks. This suggests a relatively balanced to mildly positive stance on specific equities.

## Stocks where money moved today

Observing individual stock movements within the F&O segment can highlight areas of interest for market participants.

Among the top gainers, **BHARTIARTL** saw a modest increase of 0.59%, closing at ₹1852.00, with a volume of 7,233,634 shares.

On the losing side, several prominent stocks experienced declines:
*   **INFY** was down by 3.50%, closing at ₹1000.40, with a significant volume of 24,128,263 shares, suggesting strong selling pressure.
*   **TCS** also fell by 3.17% to ₹2031.50, on a volume of 6,848,411 shares.
*   **ICICIBANK** declined by 0.89% to ₹1375.20, with 18,398,057 shares traded.
*   **SBIN** saw a similar drop of 0.89% to ₹1026.90, on a volume of 11,161,643 shares.
*   **RELIANCE** was down 0.55% to ₹1293.90, with 15,695,263 shares changing hands.

The notable declines in major IT and banking heavyweights, particularly INFY and TCS with higher volumes, indicate a sector-specific cautious sentiment today.

## What this combination historically tends to mean for tomorrow

When we combine these various signals, a nuanced picture emerges for the upcoming session. For **Nifty 50** and **Sensex**, the presence of Max Pain levels above the current spot, coupled with fresh Put writing establishing floors, historically tends to coincide with a mildly bullish bias or at least a tendency for the index to hold its ground and potentially attempt an upward move towards the Max Pain level. However, the close proximity of Call walls, especially for Nifty, suggests that any upward movement might face immediate resistance.

For **Bank Nifty**, the mixed signals, including a Call-heavy fresh flow (lower ΔPCR) alongside tightly placed Call and Put walls, have historically tended to indicate a range-bound or consolidating session. The index might find itself trading within a narrow band, as both buyers and sellers establish strong positions at nearby strike prices. The FII positioning, with a low Index futures long ratio, suggests a cautious undertone from institutional players, which historically has often coincided with subdued market breadth or a lack of strong upward momentum in the broader indices.

## How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these data points can empower you to form your own perspectives on market movements. You can typically find the Open Interest data for Nifty, Sensex, and Bank Nifty options on the National Stock Exchange (NSE) website under the "Derivatives" section. Look for "Option Chain" for the relevant expiry. Here, you'll see the Call and Put OI for various strike prices. The strike with the highest OI on the Call side and Put side will give you an idea of immediate resistance and support.

To calculate the Put-Call Ratio (PCR), simply sum up the total Put OI and total Call OI from the option chain and divide the former by the latter. Many brokerage platforms also provide this data directly. FII derivative positioning data is published daily by the NSE under the "Participant-wise Open Interest" section in their archives. By tracking these metrics consistently, you can start to identify patterns and understand how large market participants are positioning themselves, helping you gauge potential market sentiment for the next trading day.
