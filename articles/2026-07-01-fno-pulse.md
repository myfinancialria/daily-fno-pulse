# India F&O Pulse — 01 Jul 2026: what the day's options & futures positioning is whispering

## Indian Indices Show Mildly Bullish Tilt as Options Writers Build Support

**TL;DR**
*   **Nifty 50:** Options data suggests a mildly bullish outlook, with fresh put writing indicating support around current levels, despite a strong Call wall at 24000.
*   **Sensex:** A mildly bullish bias is observed, driven by significant fresh put writing today, though Max Pain sits slightly below the current spot.
*   **Bank Nifty:** The index also shows a mildly bullish tilt, with Max Pain positioned comfortably above the current spot, suggesting potential for upward movement.

## What the indices did today

### NIFTY 50
The Nifty 50 closed the day at 24,005.85, marking a gain of 0.59%. The index ended precisely at its most significant Call and Put Open Interest (OI) walls at 24000, suggesting a tug-of-war between buyers and sellers around this key psychological level.

### SENSEX
The Sensex advanced by 0.58% to close at 76,922.64. It finished within its implied option-chain range of 76000-78000, with fresh put writing today indicating a build-up of support, even as call writers positioned for a cap around 78166.

### BANK NIFTY
Bank Nifty saw the strongest gains among the three, rising 0.85% to settle at 58,033.05. The index closed significantly below its heaviest Put OI wall at 60000, which could potentially act as a strong base, while Max Pain indicates a potential upward pull.

## Decoding today's open interest

Open Interest (OI) represents the total number of outstanding derivative contracts, such as options, that have not yet been settled. For options, the highest Call OI strike typically indicates a level where many call options have been sold, acting as a potential resistance or 'ceiling' for the underlying index. Conversely, the highest Put OI strike signifies a level where many put options have been sold, often acting as a potential support or 'floor'.

Today, for Nifty 50, the heaviest Call OI was seen at 24000, exactly at the spot price, suggesting a significant hurdle for any upward move. However, the heaviest Put OI was also at 24000, indicating strong support at the same level. Fresh Call writing, which means new call options being sold, was heaviest near 24083, suggesting call sellers believe the index might struggle to move beyond this point. On the other hand, fresh Put writing, where new put options are sold, was heaviest near 23983, indicating put sellers are confident the index will hold above this level.

For Sensex, fresh Call writing was concentrated near 78166, suggesting a potential cap, while fresh Put writing was heaviest near 76433, indicating a strong floor. Bank Nifty saw its heaviest Put OI at 60000, significantly above the current spot, which could act as a strong base if the index moves higher.

## PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The Put-Call Ratio (PCR) is a sentiment indicator calculated by dividing the total Open Interest of Put options by the total Open Interest of Call options. A PCR above 1 generally suggests a bullish sentiment as more put options (often sold for support) are open, while a PCR below 1 indicates a bearish sentiment.

For Nifty 50, the overall PCR (OI) stood at 1.06, suggesting a relatively balanced options book. However, today's flow ΔPCR, which looks at only the fresh options written today, was 1.58, indicating a substantial amount of new put writing. This suggests that fresh participants are leaning towards expecting support for the index.

Sensex showed an overall PCR (OI) of 1.23, also indicating a balanced to slightly bullish book. Its today's flow ΔPCR was significantly higher at 2.16, pointing to strong fresh put writing activity, which is typically seen as a bullish signal.

Bank Nifty's overall PCR (OI) was 0.81, which is slightly below 1 and might suggest a cautious sentiment from the existing options book. However, its today's flow ΔPCR was 1.19, indicating a more balanced flow of fresh writing today, leaning slightly towards put-heavy.

## Max pain and the magnet effect

"Max Pain" refers to the strike price at which option writers, particularly those who have sold options, would experience the least financial loss if the underlying index were to expire at that level. It's often thought of as a 'magnet' for the index, especially as the expiry date approaches, as option writers tend to steer the market towards this point to maximise their profits or minimise losses.

For Nifty 50, Max Pain is at 24000, which is precisely where the index closed today. This suggests that option writers are positioned for the index to hover around this level.

Sensex's Max Pain is at 76600, which is about 0.4% below its current spot of 76,922.64. This indicates that the 'pin pull' from Max Pain is slightly downwards for Sensex.

Bank Nifty's Max Pain is at 58600, approximately 1.0% above its current spot of 58,033.05. This positioning suggests a potential upward pull for Bank Nifty towards this level.

## FII positioning — what the big money did today

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) are often considered significant players in the Indian derivatives market. Their positioning in index futures can offer insights into their broader market sentiment. Today, FIIs held an Index futures long ratio of 10.0%, meaning that only 10.0% of their total index futures contracts were long positions, while the remaining 90.0% were short. This translates to 32,476 long contracts versus 292,535 short contracts. A relatively low FII index futures long ratio has historically tended to coincide with periods where institutional players are either cautious or have significant short positions in the index futures segment, potentially indicating a hedged or bearish outlook on the broader market.

In Stock futures, FIIs had a long ratio of 53.5%, with 3,771,958 long contracts against 3,275,109 short contracts, suggesting a slightly bullish stance on individual stocks.

## Stocks where money moved today

In the F&O segment, several stocks saw notable movements today. Among the top gainers were ADANIENT, which rose by 3.54%, HINDUNILVR with a 3.01% gain, and KOTAKBANK, which advanced by 2.09% with significant volume of over 2.17 crore shares. SBIN also saw a 2.00% increase, while MARUTI gained 1.98%.

On the losing side, TCS declined by 2.41%, followed by INFY which fell 1.51% on a volume of over 1.3 crore shares. LT dropped 1.23%, HDFCBANK eased by 0.23% with a substantial volume of over 2.6 crore shares, and TITAN closed down 0.12%.

## What this combination historically tends to mean for tomorrow

Today's options data, across Nifty 50, Sensex, and Bank Nifty, points towards a mildly bullish sentiment overall. The consistent theme of strong fresh put writing, reflected in the higher today's flow ΔPCR for Nifty and Sensex, suggests that option sellers are actively building support levels. While Nifty's Max Pain is at spot and Sensex's is slightly below, Bank Nifty's Max Pain being above spot adds to the constructive outlook for that index. Historically, when options writers establish strong put bases through fresh writing, it has tended to coincide with the underlying index finding support and potentially attempting upward moves in the subsequent session. The FII index futures positioning, however, remains cautious with a low long ratio, which has historically tended to coincide with institutional participants maintaining a hedged or slightly bearish stance on the broader index. This suggests that while options data indicates short-term support, the broader institutional sentiment in futures might warrant a degree of caution.

## How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these data points can help you form your own perspective on market sentiment. You can access options Open Interest data directly from the National Stock Exchange (NSE) website under the 'Derivatives' section, specifically the 'Option Chain' for Nifty, Bank Nifty, and Sensex. Look for the total Open Interest (OI) for Calls and Puts at various strike prices. The strikes with the highest OI often indicate key resistance (for Calls) and support (for Puts) levels.

To calculate the PCR (Put-Call Ratio), simply divide the total Put OI by the total Call OI. You can also track the change in Open Interest (ΔOI) for the day to see where fresh positions are being built, which helps gauge immediate sentiment shifts. Max Pain calculators are available on many financial portals and broker platforms; these tools help identify the strike where option writers have the least pain. Combining these insights daily can provide a more informed view of where the market might be heading in the very short term, based on the positioning of options participants.
