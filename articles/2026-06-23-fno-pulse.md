# India F&O Pulse — 23 Jun 2026: what the day's options & futures positioning is whispering

Indian indices saw a down day, with F&O data suggesting mixed signals for Nifty, a bearish lean for Sensex, and a mildly bullish undertone for Bank Nifty.

**TL;DR**
*   **Nifty 50:** Derivatives data indicates a largely range-bound or mixed outlook for the upcoming session, with significant Call and Put Open Interest suggesting potential resistance and support levels.
*   **Sensex:** The positioning in Sensex options points towards a mildly bearish bias, with Call writing dominating today's activity and overall Open Interest.
*   **Bank Nifty:** Despite the market's decline, Bank Nifty's options data shows a mildly bullish undertone, primarily driven by Put writing at lower levels and a higher Max Pain point.

## Decoding today's open interest

**Open Interest (OI)** refers to the total number of outstanding derivative contracts, such as options or futures, that have not yet been settled. It provides insights into market sentiment and potential support/resistance levels. The strike price with the highest Call OI typically acts as a strong resistance level, as many traders expect the price not to rise above this point and have sold (written) Call options there. Conversely, the strike price with the highest Put OI often serves as a strong support level, as traders expect the price not to fall below it and have written Put options.

For **Nifty 50**, the heaviest Put OI is currently at 24,000, while the heaviest Call OI is observed at 25,000. Today's fresh writing activity, indicated by the change in Open Interest (ΔOI), saw a significantly larger build-up in Call OI (49.1 million contracts) compared to Put OI (23.8 million contracts). This suggests that more new Call options were sold today than Put options, particularly around the 24,000 and higher strikes, indicating that option writers believe the upside might be limited.

In **Sensex**, the option chain shows significant Put OI at 75,000 and Call OI at 79,000. Today's aggregate flow saw a substantial build-up in Net Call OI (14.7 million contracts) versus Net Put OI (3.8 million contracts). This heavy fresh Call writing, particularly around and above the current spot levels, suggests that option writers are positioning for the index to face resistance and potentially struggle to move higher.

For **Bank Nifty**, the option chain indicates strong Put OI at 55,000 and Call OI at 58,000. Today's activity showed notable fresh Call writing near the 57,800 strike, suggesting that option sellers anticipate this level to act as a cap. However, there was also significant fresh Put writing around 56,966, indicating that some traders are willing to sell Put options at these lower levels, expecting them to hold as support.

## PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The **Put-Call Ratio (PCR)** is a widely used indicator that measures the total Open Interest of Put options relative to Call options. A PCR above 1 generally suggests a bullish sentiment, as there are more Put options outstanding (often sold by those expecting prices to rise or remain stable), while a PCR below 1 typically indicates a bearish sentiment, with more Call options outstanding (often sold by those expecting prices to fall or remain stable).

For **Nifty 50**, the PCR based on total Open Interest (PCR(OI)) stands at 0.77, which is considered relatively balanced. However, today's flow, measured by the change in Open Interest (ΔPCR), was 0.49. This lower ΔPCR indicates that fresh Call writing dominated today's activity, suggesting a slight bearish tilt from today's new positioning.

**Sensex** recorded a PCR(OI) of 0.6, which is below 0.7 and generally points towards a bearish sentiment, implying that Call options significantly outnumber Put options. The ΔPCR for today's flow was even lower at 0.26, reinforcing the bearish signal as fresh Call writing heavily outweighed fresh Put writing, indicating that option writers are positioning for limited upside.

**Bank Nifty** has a PCR(OI) of 0.72, which suggests a relatively balanced book. Interestingly, today's flow saw a ΔPCR of -0.59. While this negative value might seem unusual, it reflects a mixed or balanced flow, where fresh Put writing was also significant, offsetting some of the Call writing, leading to a less clear directional signal from today's new positions.

## Max pain and the magnet effect

**Max Pain** is the strike price at which option writers (those who sell options) would experience the minimum aggregate loss if the underlying asset were to expire at that price. It is often seen as a "magnet" for the index, especially as expiry approaches, as option writers tend to try and steer the price towards this level to maximise their profits or minimise losses.

For **Nifty 50**, the Max Pain level is at 24,100, which is approximately 1.2% above its current spot price. This suggests that option writers, on aggregate, would prefer Nifty to settle closer to 24,100 at expiry.

**Sensex** has its Max Pain point at 77,300, sitting about 1.4% above its current spot. This indicates a potential upward pull towards this level as expiry nears, from the perspective of option writers.

**Bank Nifty's** Max Pain is at 57,800, which is around 1.1% above its current spot price. This higher Max Pain level, combined with fresh Put writing at lower strikes, suggests a potential for the index to gravitate upwards towards this point.

## FII positioning — what the big money did today

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) play a significant role in the Indian derivatives market. Their positioning can offer insights into broader market sentiment. Today, FIIs held a relatively low long ratio of **13.1%** in Index futures, with 40,727 long contracts against 269,288 short contracts. Historically, a consistently low FII long ratio in index futures has tended to coincide with periods of cautious or bearish market sentiment.

In Stock futures, FIIs maintained a more balanced long ratio of **53.9%**, with 4,128,938 long contracts and 3,533,896 short contracts. This suggests a relatively neutral to slightly bullish stance on individual stocks compared to their more pronounced bearish lean in index futures.

## How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these market signals daily can empower you to form your own informed perspective. You can start by looking at the **Open Interest (OI)** data, which is readily available on the NSE website under the "Derivatives" section. Specifically, check the "Option Chain" for Nifty, Sensex, and Bank Nifty. Observe the strike prices with the highest Call OI and Put OI – these are your potential resistance and support levels.

Next, look at the "Change in Open Interest" (ΔOI) for the day. A significant increase in Call OI at higher strikes suggests Call writers are active, expecting the market to stay below those levels. Conversely, a surge in Put OI at lower strikes indicates Put writers are providing support. You can also calculate the **Put-Call Ratio (PCR)** by dividing the total Put OI by the total Call OI. A PCR below 1 often suggests a bearish bias, while above 1 leans bullish. Finally, keep an eye on the FII derivative statistics, also available on the NSE website, to gauge the sentiment of institutional players. Combining these data points daily can help you build a more comprehensive understanding of market positioning.
