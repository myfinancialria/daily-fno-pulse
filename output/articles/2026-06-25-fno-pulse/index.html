Decoding today's open interest

Open Interest (OI) in options refers to the total number of outstanding contracts that have not yet been settled. It provides a snapshot of market participants' positioning and expectations. When we look at the highest Call OI strike, it generally indicates a level where Call option sellers (also known as "writers") believe the underlying index is unlikely to rise above. This effectively forms a resistance level. Conversely, the highest Put OI strike represents a level where Put option sellers expect the index to find support and not fall below, thus acting as a floor.

For NIFTY 50, the heaviest Call OI is observed at 25,000, which is significantly above the current spot price, while the heaviest Put OI stands at 24,000. Today's fresh writing saw a substantial net build-up in Call OI (24.1 million contracts) compared to Put OI (0.6 million contracts). This indicates that new sellers were more active in writing Calls, particularly near 23,766 for Puts and higher strikes for Calls, suggesting an expectation of limited upside or consolidation, even as Put sellers are establishing floors.

In SENSEX, the maximum Call OI is concentrated at 77,100, very close to the spot price, while the heaviest Put OI is at 77,000. Today's fresh Call writing was particularly heavy near 77,266, suggesting that option sellers are positioning for the index to face resistance around these levels. However, fresh Put writing was also seen near 77,100, indicating some expectation of support at or around current levels.

For BANK NIFTY, the option chain shows significant Call OI at 60,000 and Put OI at 56,000, outlining a broad range. Today's heaviest fresh Call writing was observed near 58,900, suggesting that option sellers anticipate the index might struggle to move significantly higher than this level. On the Put side, fresh writing was concentrated near 57,800, indicating that some participants expect this to be a potential support zone.

PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The Put-Call Ratio (PCR) is a widely watched indicator that compares the total Open Interest of Put options to the total Open Interest of Call options. A PCR(OI) value above 1 typically suggests a relatively bullish sentiment, as more Puts are outstanding, indicating more participants are selling Puts (expecting prices to rise) or buying Puts (hedging against a fall). A value below 1 often points to a relatively bearish sentiment, with more Calls outstanding.

For NIFTY 50, the PCR(OI) stands at 0.88, indicating a relatively balanced book with a slight lean towards Call options. However, the PCR based on today's fresh flow (ΔOI) is 0.02, which is quite low and suggests that today's new option writing activity was heavily skewed towards Calls. This combination implies that while the overall positioning is somewhat balanced, the fresh activity reflects a more cautious or bearish sentiment from option writers for the immediate future.

The SENSEX also has a PCR(OI) of 0.88, similar to Nifty, suggesting a balanced overall options book. However, its PCR based on today's fresh flow (ΔOI) is 0.6. While higher than Nifty's, this still indicates that fresh Call writing outpaced fresh Put writing today, contributing to a mildly bearish signal from the day's activity.

BANK NIFTY shows a PCR(OI) of 0.93, suggesting a nearly balanced options book. The PCR based on today's fresh flow (ΔOI) is -0.08, indicating a mixed or slightly Call-heavy flow for the day, which aligns with a neutral to mildly bearish interpretation from the day's writing activity.

Max pain and the magnet effect

"Max Pain" refers to the strike price at which option writers (those who sell options) would experience the least amount of financial loss if the underlying asset were to expire at that specific price. It's often considered a "magnet" for the index, especially as the weekly or monthly expiry approaches, as option writers tend to adjust their positions to minimise their collective losses.

For NIFTY 50, the Max Pain strike is at 24,150, which is approximately 0.4% above its current spot price of 24,056.00. Historically, when Max Pain is above the spot price, it has tended to coincide with a slight upward pull on the index as expiry nears.

SENSEX has its Max Pain strike at 77,200, which is very close to its current spot price of 77,100.47. When Max Pain is at or very near the spot price, it often suggests that the index might consolidate around these levels, with no strong directional pull from this indicator.

For BANK NIFTY, the Max Pain strike is at 58,000, which is about 0.3% below its current spot price of 58,177.05. Historically, when Max Pain is below the spot price, it has tended to coincide with a slight downward pull on the index as expiry approaches.

How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these market signals can empower you to interpret daily movements more effectively. You can access options Open Interest data directly from the National Stock Exchange (NSE) website under the "Derivatives" section, specifically the "Option Chain" for Nifty, Bank Nifty, and Sensex. Many brokerage platforms also provide this data in an easily digestible format.

To interpret it, first, look at the highest Call OI and Put OI strikes to identify potential resistance and support levels. Then, observe the "Change in OI" column to see where fresh buying or selling (writing) activity is concentrated for the day. Calculate the PCR by dividing total Put OI by total Call OI. For FII data, the NSE publishes participant-wise Open Interest data daily, which can be downloaded and analysed. Combining these data points—OI walls, fresh writing, PCR, Max Pain, and FII positioning—can help you form a more informed perspective on the market's underlying sentiment and potential short-term direction, always remembering that these are historical tendencies and not guarantees.
