India F&O Pulse — 29 Jun 2026: what the day's options & futures positioning is whispering

Index Futures and Options Data Point to a Cautious, Range-Bound Market for Nifty and Sensex, While Bank Nifty Shows a Mildly Bearish Tilt.

TL;DR

  • NIFTY 50: The data suggests a range-bound to mixed outlook, with significant call writing indicating potential resistance above current levels, but max pain offering an upward pull.
  • SENSEX: Similar to Nifty, Sensex appears poised for a range-bound session, with a strong call wall near spot suggesting an immediate ceiling.
  • BANK NIFTY: Today's options activity indicates a mildly bearish bias, with fresh call writing outweighing put writing and the index closing lower.

What the indices did today

The NIFTY 50 closed the day at 23,946.25, registering a decline of 0.46%. The index settled just below its heaviest Put Open Interest (OI) wall at 24,000, suggesting this level might act as immediate support, while the 25,000 mark remains a significant overhead resistance according to the option chain.

The SENSEX also saw a dip, closing at 76,728.37, down 0.48% for the session. The index finished very close to its heaviest Call OI wall at 77,000, indicating that this level could present a challenge for upward movement, with the broader option chain suggesting a range between 74,000 and 77,000.

The BANK NIFTY experienced a more pronounced decline, shedding 0.77% to close at 57,727.35. The index concluded the day near its current At-The-Money (ATM) strike of 57,700, with the option chain implying a wider trading range between 56,000 and 60,000.

Decoding today's open interest

Open Interest (OI) represents the total number of outstanding derivative contracts, like options, that have not yet been settled. When we look at the highest Call OI strike, it generally signifies a level where a large number of call options have been sold (written), suggesting that option sellers (often referred to as "writers") believe the underlying index may not rise above this point. Conversely, the highest Put OI strike indicates a level where many put options have been written, implying that put sellers expect the index to find support and not fall below this level. These levels often act as significant resistance and support points, respectively.

For the NIFTY 50, today's fresh writing activity saw a substantial build-up in Call OI, particularly near the 24,366 strike. This suggests that call sellers are positioning themselves for the index to remain capped around or below this level. On the Put side, fresh writing was heaviest near 23,816, indicating that put sellers anticipate this level to act as a floor. The aggregate flow for Nifty showed a much larger net build-up in Call OI (66.3 million contracts) compared to Put OI (13.8 million contracts) today, which generally suggests a more cautious or bearish sentiment from option writers.

On the SENSEX, today's fresh Put writing was most concentrated near 75,233, implying that put sellers expect this level to hold as support. The heaviest Call OI wall for Sensex is currently at 77,000, which is quite close to the spot price, indicating a potential ceiling in the immediate term.

For BANK NIFTY, fresh Call writing was most significant near the 58,500 strike, suggesting that call sellers do not expect the index to move substantially above this point. Simultaneously, fresh Put writing was observed around 57,666, which put sellers may see as a potential support zone. The overall flow for Bank Nifty also showed a clear dominance of net Call OI build (3.6 million contracts) over Put OI build (0.7 million contracts), reinforcing a cautious outlook from option writers.

PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The Put-Call Ratio (PCR) is a widely watched indicator that compares the total Open Interest of put options to the total Open Interest of call options. It provides insight into the overall sentiment of option traders. A PCR value above 1 typically indicates that more put options are being written than call options, which can be interpreted as a bullish signal (as put writers profit if prices rise). Conversely, a PCR below 1 suggests more call options are being written, often seen as a bearish signal.

For the NIFTY 50, the PCR based on total Open Interest (PCR(OI)) stands at 0.72, which is generally considered balanced to slightly bearish. More significantly, today's flow PCR (ΔPCR), which only considers the fresh options written today, is at a low 0.21. This indicates that today's fresh options writing was heavily skewed towards calls, suggesting a strong bearish sentiment from option writers for the immediate session.

The SENSEX has a PCR(OI) of 0.78, also indicating a relatively balanced to slightly bearish book. Its ΔPCR for today's flow is 0.61, again pointing towards a greater proportion of fresh call writing compared to put writing, suggesting a cautious sentiment for the day.

For BANK NIFTY, the PCR(OI) is 0.84, which is also in the balanced to slightly bearish territory. However, its ΔPCR for today's flow is a very low 0.20, signifying that today's fresh options writing was overwhelmingly dominated by call options, which historically tends to coincide with a bearish outlook from option writers.

Max pain and the magnet effect

"Max pain" refers to the strike price at which option writers (sellers) would experience the least aggregate loss if the underlying asset expires at that level. It's often viewed as a "magnet" for the expiry price, as option writers, who collectively hold a significant portion of the open interest, tend to manage their positions to minimise their overall losses. This "magnet effect" can become stronger as the expiry date approaches.

For the NIFTY 50, the max pain strike is at 24,200. This level is approximately 1.1% above the current spot price, suggesting a potential upward pull towards this level as expiry nears.

The SENSEX has its max pain strike at 77,200, which is about 0.6% above its current spot price. This also indicates a potential upward bias towards this level.

For BANK NIFTY, the max pain strike is at 58,000, sitting approximately 0.5% above the current spot price, suggesting a gentle upward pull.

FII positioning — what the big money did today

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) are significant participants in the Indian derivatives market, and their positioning can offer insights into broader market sentiment. Today, FIIs held a long ratio of 17.0% in Index futures, with 60,489 long contracts against 294,940 short contracts. This relatively low long ratio historically tends to coincide with a cautious or bearish outlook from these large participants. In contrast, their Stock futures long ratio stands at 54.6%, indicating a slightly more balanced to bullish stance in individual stock futures.

Stocks where money moved today

In the F&O watchlist, some individual stocks saw notable movements today. Among the top gainers, HDFCBANK climbed 0.33% to ₹798.90, with a volume of over 4.6 crore shares. ITC also saw a modest gain of 0.24% to ₹290.70.

On the losing side, KOTAKBANK experienced a significant drop of 3.30% to ₹395.50, with substantial volume of over 3.7 crore shares. ADANIENT declined by 2.49% to ₹2962.50, and MARUTI fell 2.42% to ₹13412.00. AXISBANK and RELIANCE also closed lower, down 1.48% and 1.30% respectively.

What this combination historically tends to mean for tomorrow

When we synthesise today's data, a picture of caution emerges, particularly from the options writing activity. The significant fresh Call writing and the low daily Put-Call Ratios for all three indices suggest that option writers are largely anticipating limited upside and potential resistance in the immediate term. While the max pain levels for all indices are slightly above their current spot prices, offering a gentle upward pull, the strong Call OI walls and the dominance of fresh call writing indicate that these upward movements might face hurdles.

Historically, when such a combination of heavy fresh call writing, low daily PCR, and a low FII index futures long ratio is observed, the subsequent trading session has tended to coincide with either a range-bound movement with a negative bias or continued downside pressure, as option writers appear to be positioning for a cap on prices. The "range-bound / mixed" bias for Nifty and Sensex, coupled with a "mildly bearish" bias for Bank Nifty from the aggregate signals, reinforces this cautious outlook.

How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these market signals can empower you to interpret daily F&O data. To start, you can access Open Interest data directly from the National Stock Exchange (NSE) website under the "Derivatives" section, specifically the "Option Chain" for Nifty, Bank Nifty, and Sensex. Many brokerage platforms also provide this data in an easily digestible format.

Look for the strike prices with the highest Call OI and Put OI – these are your potential resistance and support levels. Pay attention to the "Change in OI" column to identify where fresh buying or selling (writing) activity occurred today. Calculate the PCR by dividing total Put OI by total Call OI. For FII positioning, the NSE publishes daily participant-wise Open Interest data which you can download and analyse. By consistently observing these data points, you can begin to discern patterns and understand how large participants are positioning themselves in the market, helping you form your own informed perspective.
