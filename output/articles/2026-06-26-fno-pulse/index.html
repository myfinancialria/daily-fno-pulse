India F&O Pulse — 26 Jun 2026: what the day's options & futures positioning is whispering

Options Data Suggests Cautious Optimism for Nifty, Mixed Signals for Sensex, and a Bearish Tilt for Bank Nifty

TL;DR

  • Nifty 50 options data points towards a mildly bullish bias for the upcoming session, with support appearing robust.
  • Sensex positioning indicates a largely range-bound or mixed outlook, with both strong support and resistance levels in proximity.
  • Bank Nifty options data suggests a mildly bearish sentiment, with the "max pain" point below the current spot level.

What the indices did today

The Nifty 50 closed today at 24,056.00, marking a modest gain of 0.14%. The index ended the day just above its immediate Put option wall at 24,000, while a significant Call option wall remains further up at 25,000, suggesting a potential floor forming nearby.

The Sensex also saw a slight uptick, closing at 77,100.47, up 0.14%. The index finds itself nestled between strong option walls, with the heaviest Call and Put Open Interest both clustered around the 77,500 mark, indicating a tight potential trading range.

Meanwhile, the Bank Nifty finished the session at 58,177.05, with a marginal gain of 0.05%. The index closed above its "max pain" point of 58,000, but with significant Call option writing observed at lower strikes, suggesting potential resistance overhead.

Decoding today's open interest

Open Interest (OI) in options refers to the total number of outstanding contracts that have not yet been settled. It gives us a snapshot of where market participants, particularly option writers (sellers), are positioning themselves.

The highest Call Open Interest (Call OI) strike typically acts as a resistance level. This is because Call writers profit if the price stays below their strike, so they would likely defend these levels. For Nifty 50, the heaviest Call OI is at 25,000, suggesting a significant ceiling. For Sensex, the 77,500 strike holds the highest Call OI, indicating a strong resistance point. Bank Nifty sees its highest Call OI at 60,000.

Conversely, the highest Put Open Interest (Put OI) strike often serves as a support level. Put writers profit if the price stays above their strike, making them inclined to protect these levels. Nifty 50's strongest Put OI base is at 24,000. Sensex also has its heaviest Put OI at 77,500, which is interesting as it coincides with the Call wall. Bank Nifty's most substantial Put OI is at 56,000.

Looking at today's fresh writing, which refers to new contracts added during the day, we can see where new convictions are emerging. For Nifty 50, the heaviest fresh Call writing was observed near the 22,600 strike. This suggests that Call sellers are expecting the index to stay capped above these levels, potentially indicating a belief that the upside is limited from a broader perspective. For Sensex, the heaviest fresh Call writing was near 74,200, similarly implying that Call sellers anticipate a cap. Bank Nifty saw its heaviest fresh Call writing near 55,300, again suggesting that option writers are comfortable selling calls at these lower strikes, expecting the index to remain above them.

PCR — what the put-call ratio is whispering

The Put-Call Ratio (PCR) is a widely watched indicator that compares the total Open Interest of Put options to Call options. A PCR(OI) value above 1 typically suggests more Put writing than Call writing, often interpreted as a bullish sentiment (as Put writers expect prices to rise or stay stable). A value below 1, with more Call writing, can signal a bearish sentiment (as Call writers expect prices to fall or stay stable).

For Nifty 50, the PCR(OI) stands at 0.88. This value, slightly below 1, suggests a relatively balanced book with a slight lean towards Call writing, indicating a neutral-to-slightly cautious sentiment from option writers. The PCR for today's flow (ΔOI) is 0, implying a mixed or balanced flow of new Put and Call writing during the session.

Sensex also shows a PCR(OI) of 0.91, again indicating a largely balanced options book with a minor tilt towards Call writing. Its daily flow ΔPCR is 0, suggesting new activity was also balanced.

Bank Nifty's PCR(OI) is 0.92, similar to the other indices, pointing to a balanced options structure with a slight inclination towards Call writing. Its daily flow ΔPCR is also 0, reflecting mixed fresh activity.

Overall, the PCR values across all three indices hover close to 1, suggesting that option writers are not taking overwhelmingly directional bets based on the overall open interest, and today's fresh activity was largely balanced.

Max pain and the magnet effect

"Max pain" is the strike price at which option writers would experience the minimum aggregate loss if the index were to expire at that level. It's often considered a magnetic point, especially as expiry approaches, as option writers might try to steer the underlying index towards this point to maximise their collective profits or minimise losses.

For Nifty 50, the max pain point is at 24,150, which is approximately 0.4% above its current spot price of 24,056. This suggests a potential upward pull for the index towards this level as expiry nears.

Sensex has its max pain at 77,500, about 0.5% above its current spot of 77,100.47. This also indicates a potential upward gravitational pull towards this strike.

Bank Nifty, however, shows its max pain at 58,000, which is around 0.3% below its spot price of 58,177.05. This suggests a potential downward pull for the Bank Nifty towards the 58,000 level.

The closer an index is to its expiry, the stronger this "magnet effect" of max pain tends to be.

FII positioning — what the big money did today

Foreign Institutional Investors (FIIs) are significant players in the Indian derivatives market, and their positioning can offer insights into broader market sentiment.

As of yesterday (June 25, 2026), FIIs held a relatively low long ratio in Index futures, standing at 15.7%. This means that out of their total Index futures positions, only 15.7% were long contracts, while the majority were short. Historically, such a low long ratio in index futures has tended to coincide with a cautious or even bearish stance from FIIs on the broader market.

In Stock futures, FIIs showed a long ratio of 54.4%, indicating a slightly bullish bias in their stock-specific bets. It's important to remember that this data reflects yesterday's positioning, as today's FII derivative archive from NSE is typically published with a slight delay.

Stocks where money moved today

In the F&O segment, certain stocks saw notable price and volume action today.

Among the top gainers, MARUTI led the pack, rising by 3.75% to ₹13,745.00 with a volume of 981,556 shares. SBIN also saw a positive move, up 1.04% to ₹1,045.40, with a significant volume of over 1.6 crore shares. ICICIBANK followed closely, gaining 1.01% to ₹1,387.50, also with high volumes exceeding 1.7 crore shares.

On the losing side, INFY declined by 1.46% to ₹1,041.20, with volumes of nearly 1.6 crore shares. BHARTIARTL was down 1.42% to ₹1,850.70, while BAJFINANCE fell 1.06% to ₹980.40, with volumes of over 1.2 crore shares. These movements are purely observational and reflect where trading activity was concentrated today.

What this combination historically tends to mean for tomorrow

Synthesising the various signals, the overall picture for tomorrow's session appears to be one of mixed sentiment, with some nuances across the indices.

For Nifty 50, the mildly bullish bias derived from the 8-vote check, coupled with a max pain point above spot and a strong Put base at 24,000, historically tends to coincide with the index attempting to hold its immediate support and potentially drift higher. However, fresh Call writing at lower levels suggests some caution from option sellers.

Sensex signals are largely range-bound or mixed. The proximity of both Call and Put walls at 77,500, along with a balanced PCR, historically tends to suggest that the index might consolidate within a defined range. While max pain is slightly higher, the strong Call wall could act as a significant barrier.

Bank Nifty exhibits a mildly bearish bias. A max pain point below spot, combined with fresh Call writing at lower strikes, historically tends to coincide with the index facing potential downward pressure or struggling to sustain higher levels. The balanced PCR, however, suggests no extreme directional conviction from overall option writers.

The FIIs' cautious stance in index futures, historically, has tended to coincide with a lack of strong upward momentum in the broader market, even if individual stocks show strength. When these signals align as they have today, the next session has historically tended to see indices reacting to these established option walls and the gravitational pull of max pain, often leading to a session of consolidation or a slight directional bias as indicated by the individual index readings.

How to read this yourself daily

Understanding these market signals can empower you to interpret daily movements better. You can start by looking at the Open Interest data, which is freely available on the National Stock Exchange (NSE) website under the "Derivatives" section, specifically the "Option Chain" for Nifty, Bank Nifty, and Sensex (via the BSE's derivatives section for Sensex). Many brokerage platforms also provide this data in an easily digestible format.

When you look at the option chain, identify the strikes with the highest Call OI and highest Put OI. These are your immediate resistance and support levels, respectively. Then, observe where new Open Interest (ΔOI) is being added for both Calls and Puts. Significant fresh Call writing at a particular strike might suggest a new resistance, while fresh Put writing could indicate new support. Calculate the PCR by dividing total Put OI by total Call OI; a value closer to 1 suggests neutrality, while deviations can hint at bullish (above 1) or bearish (below 1) sentiment. Finally, locate the "max pain" strike, which some platforms calculate for you, or you can estimate it by finding the strike with the least aggregate Call and Put OI. Combining these data points daily can help you form a more informed perspective on potential market movements.
